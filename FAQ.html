Q1: What datasets are included in TRExplorer?

Population allele frequencies and other annations are being added from an increasing number of sources.

Assembly-based datasets
  • Telomere-to-Telomere [T2T]: 78 diploid T2T assembly-to-hg38 alignments, genotyped using Dipcall v0.3.
Long-read datasets
  • All of Us project [AoU]: 1,027 PacBio HiFi genomes from African American participants, genotyped with TRGT.
  • Human Pan-genome Reference Consortium [HPRC]: 100 PacBio HiFi genomes, genotyped with TRGT.
Short-read datasets
  • TenK10K Phase 1 [10k10k]: 1,925 Illumina (150 bp paired end sequencing; 30x mean coverage; aligned to hg38) genomes from European ancestry individuals, genotyped with ExpansionHunter v5.
  • 1000 Genomes Project [1KGP]: 2,504 Illumina (101 bp paired end sequencing; 7.4x mean depth; aligned to hg38) genomes from individuals of diverse ancestry, characterized with STR-Finder as described in the Illumina STR generation docs.


Q2: Can I contribute data to TRExplorer?

If you are a study investigator and are interested in contributing tandem repeat genotypes or annotations to the TRExplorer resource, please email Ben Weisburd at weisburd@broadinstitute.org


Q3: How do I cite TRExplorer?

If you use TRExplorer in your work, please cite the following preprint:

Weisburd B, Dolzhenko E, Bennett MF, Danzi MC, English A, Hiatt L, Tanudisastro H, Kurtas NE, Jam HZ, Brand H, Sedlazeck FJ, Gymrek M, Dashnow H, Eberle MA, Rehm HL. Defining a tandem repeat catalog and variation clusters for genome-wide analyses and population databases. bioRxiv 2024.10.04.615514; https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.10.04.615514
